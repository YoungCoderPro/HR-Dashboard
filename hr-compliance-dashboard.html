How to use this score

▼

The HR Compliance Burden Score answers one question: how much HR work does operating in this state actually require? A higher score means more laws to follow, more filings to make, and more ways to accidentally violate something — not that the state is bad, just that it takes more effort to stay compliant.

For StoicLane — deal evaluation
When evaluating an acquisition, the score tells you upfront how complex the target’s HR environment is. A business operating entirely in Texas (Tier A) has a very different compliance cost structure than one with headcount in California and New York (Tier D). Factor it into diligence and 100-day integration planning.
For portfolio HR managers
If your company operates in 10 states, you can’t treat them all equally. The score tells you which 2–3 states need active monthly attention (PFML filings, pay range postings, AI audits) vs. which ones you can review quarterly. Higher score = more calendar items, not more legal risk per se.
For ArmHR — PEO pricing and risk
As a PEO, ArmHR assumes HR liability on behalf of clients. A client with employees in Tier D states (CA, NY, WA, NJ) carries meaningfully more compliance exposure than one in Tier A states. The score supports risk-based service pricing and flagging which client relationships need more hands-on support.
What the score does not tell you: It measures administrative burden, not legal risk or cost. A Tier A state can still expose you to significant federal liability (FMLA, EEOC, FLSA). The score is a prioritization tool, not a legal opinion. Always verify current requirements with counsel before acting.
How scores are calculated: Six dimensions are weighted and combined — Minimum Wage gap from federal floor (15%), PFML program complexity (20%), Pay Transparency requirements (15%), Workers Comp structure (15%), Sick Leave mandates (15%), and Anti-Discrimination / AI law breadth (20%). Each data point is cited to a source tier: T1 = primary statute or state agency · T2 = enacted bill or formal guidance · T3 = aggregator such as SHRM or BrightMine (verify against primary before acting).
